💗What colors and patterns do you offer? 

The Dachshund can come in many colors and patterns. We will list an ESTIMATED description of their color/pattern under each puppy for sale tab. Of course as they grow this could change. We can not control color and patterns especially when they are listed as newborns/young pups as color will change all the way into adult hood. For Example, Alot of pups that look like reds but as pups grow they end up more of a cream color, and patterns/dappling changes and develops and moves as they grow. Some dogs go lighter, some go darker.

If you are set on a specific color/pup then we ask that you wait to place a deposit until 6-8 weeks when they have grown more into their color and coat and you can see if that color is for you. We make no guarantees on color as it is uncontrollable how puppies grow. Each breeder has their own opinion on coloring as well. 

A dogs coloring will change naturally as they grow into adulthood and this is due to lots of factors... genetics, hair type, loss of puppy fur into their adult coat, pigmentation, allergies, hormonal changes, environmental changes, hair care, nutrition and so on. Color changes should be expected as a puppy grows. We will never guarantee a dog's permanent coat color for these reasons.

💛What size will my dog be?

We breed miniature dachshunds and tweenie size dachshunds. We do not breed toy, or micro dachshunds. I personally think the smaller they are the uglier they are, and they don't have good bone structure and you are sacrificing quality for the size. (Sorry but it's the truth!) 

Also Keep in mind that Miniature Dachshunds were originally bred down from standard dachshund lines and there are lots of generations of dogs lines that factor into a dogs size that are not controllable. Sometimes they will bring a smaller size dog and sometimes they are a larger size dog, and sometimes you get a mix of sizes. 

NO HONEST BREEDER WILL EVER GUARANTEE A SIZE! It is uncontrollable and I will never guarantee a size!

Breeders can have two 10 pound miniatures mate and produce puppies in a litter that at 1 year of age have adult weights of 6lbs -18 lbs all from the same litter. I have personally seen it happen, and it has a lot to do with each dogs past genetics/breeding lines, BUT ALSO the puppy’s  lifestyle, growth factors, environment, exercise, immune system, and feedings. There are too many factors that can affect the size of a puppy during their first year of growth. 

As an example…It’s like saying what size will a families kids be, based off the two parents….kids could be tall or short, fat or skinny, or you could end up with a mix. No one can predict this!

💛Why buy a dog from a Texas State License Breeder?

Texas law now requires breeders to obtain a license from the Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation for anyone who breeds 5 or more adult intact female dogs. There are many breeders out there that are NOT licensed and breeding dogs illegally however we have been inspected by the State of Texas and we are required by law to follow the state guidelines for our housing, records as well as animal care. All of our dogs receive annual exams and exceptional care!
